What is a funeral plan?

Having a funeral plan is a way to pay for your funeral in advance. This means whoever arranges your funeral won't have to pay and they'll know what your funeral wishes were.

  • Peace of mind knowing your funeral is paid for
  • People know your funeral wishes

What's not included in a funeral plan

Our funeral plans include all the elements needed for a funeral. They're not suitable if you want a military funeral or burial at sea.

It's also not possible for us to guarantee certain religious requirements with a funeral plan, such as arranging a cremation with 24 hours.

We will always do our best to follow wishes you leave within your funeral plan. For more information speak to a local funeral director.

If you have a terminal illness

A funeral plan might not be right for you if you have a terminal illness and a prognosis of up to 12 months. This is because funeral plans are priced differently than today's funerals and you might end up paying more.

We can help you make plans for your funeral by speaking to one of our funeral directors.

Check if you qualify for a plan

To buy a plan you must:

  • Be over 18
  • Buy the plan for yourself. If you need to buy a plan on behalf of someone and you have power of attorney, please visit your local funeral home.
  • Not have made any other provisions for your funeral to be paid for, for example taken out an Over 50s plan or have a life insurance plan for funeral expenses.
